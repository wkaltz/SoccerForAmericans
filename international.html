Welcome to an Introduction to International Soccer

Most countries around the world have their own national team, comprised of players who are from or have roots for their country. A lot of people consider international football to be the highest level of football, as it is the best of the best from every country. Most people, regardless of their knowledge of football or not, have heard of the World Cup. Every four years, countries from around the world compete in a tournament to try and win the World Cup. Like most football tournaments, you have to qualify for the knockout rounds. Traditionally, there are eight groups of four teams. Each team plays each other once, and the two teams with the most points will move on to the knockout rounds. They would then go on to a knockout tournament, in which the last team standing wins the trophy. However, with the upcoming World Cup hosted by

the United States, Canada, and Mexico, FIFA has opted to introduce a new system. There will instead be twelve groups of four, with the top two teams and the eight best teams who finished in third moving on to the knockout round. Instead of them moving on to a round of sixteen, they are moving on to a round of thirty two, adding a whole different aspect to the cup. This not only gives more countries an opportunity to compete at the most watched tournament in the world, but it also gives us fans more football to watch. What most fans don't realize is that each

continent (except for Antarctica) have their own tournaments. Every country that has a football team on each respective continent can compete in a continental tournament to fight to be the best international team in the continent. Some of these competitions include the Euros for Europe, AFCON in Africa, the CONCACAF Nations League in North America, the Copa America for South America, and the AFC Asian Cup for Asia. Winning these tournaments not only prove you are the best team in the continent, but some competitions also hold some serious weight in qualifying for the World Cup. These competitions are often extremely important for countries around the world, allowing the citizens of each country to come together and support one team.
